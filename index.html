Privacy Policy — Alps Summits

Last updated: 14 June 2026

Alps Summits (“the app”) is designed to respect your privacy. This policy explains what the app does — and does not — do with information.

Summary

Alps Summits does not collect, transmit, or sell any personal data. There are no accounts, no analytics, no advertising, and no third-party tracking.

Information stored on your device

Everything you create in the app — the summits you mark as climbed, dates, ratings and notes — is stored locally on your device using Apple’s on-device database (SwiftData). This information never leaves your device and is never sent to us or anyone else. Deleting the app removes this data.

Pro features — including “Summits in view” (which 4000ers are visible from a peak) and “Routes by difficulty” — are computed entirely on your device from this bundled reference data and your own records. They send nothing anywhere and require no account or connection.

Purchases

Alps Summits offers an optional one-time “Pro” purchase and an optional tip. These are processed entirely by Apple through the App Store. We never receive or store your payment details. Your purchase entitlement is verified on your device by Apple’s StoreKit; we operate no servers.

Maps

The map is provided by Apple Maps (MapKit). When you use the map, Apple may process map requests in accordance with Apple’s Privacy Policy. Alps Summits does not request your location and does not track where you are.

Summit data

Summit names, elevations and coordinates are adapted from Wikipedia, available under the Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike license. This is public reference data and contains no information about you.

Children

The app is suitable for all ages and collects no data from anyone, including children.

Your control

Because all your data stays on your device, you are always in control. To erase everything, delete the app from your device.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a new “Last updated” date.
